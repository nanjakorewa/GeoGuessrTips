    <h2 class="section-title">{{% i18n "how-to-distinguish" %}}</h2>
    <ul class="rule-list">
        <li><span class="quiz">Blue</span> mailboxes sit on street corners.</li>
        <li>Speed-limit signs spell out <span class="quiz">SPEED LIMIT</span>.</li>
        <li>If the inverted triangle sign says YIELD it is the <span class="quiz">United States</span>; when the text is missing it is usually <span class="quiz">Canada</span>.</li>
        <li><span class="quiz">Double yellow</span> centre lines are standard across both the United States and Canada.</li>
        <li>It is common to see rows of garbage bins set out in front of homes.</li>
        <li>Speed limits may be written in <span class="quiz">MPH</span>.</li>
    </ul>

    <h4 class="section-title">Clues for narrowing down the state</h2>
    <p>Decide first whether you are on an island, then use the licence plates, state-highway shields, the type of mountains and vegetation, how flat the terrain is, and the feel of the houses to narrow down the location. Some states have very distinctive clues.</p>
    <ul class="rule-list">
        <li>① Whether cars have front plates and the plate colour can narrow down the state<a href="https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1P7LRYb13phYIYWlu4KSXxTosMGV44tHFU_EgqQvEXVQ/edit#gid=0">[Reference]</a>{{% by "rumilb" "twitch" %}}. Start by memorising which states require a front plate.
            <ul>
                <li><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_license_plate_designs_and_serial_formats">List of US licence plates (Wikipedia)</a></li>
                <li class="no-evidence">States with “new” in the plate name often look yellowish{{% by "niwaisound" "twitch" %}}.</li>
                <li>Characteristic colours by state
                    <ul>
                        <li>Mostly black → <span class="quiz">Delaware</span></li>
                        <li>Mostly green → <span class="quiz">Vermont</span></li>
                        <li>Deep orange → <span class="quiz">New York</span></li>
                        <li>All yellow → <span class="quiz">Alaska</span></li>
                        <li>Pale yellow overall or on both sides → <span class="quiz">Mississippi</span></li>
                        <li>White background with an orange panel on the right → <span class="quiz">Utah</span></li>
                        <li>White background with an orange fruit in the centre → <span class="quiz">Florida / Georgia</span></li>
                        <li>White background with a green tree in the centre → <span class="quiz">Oregon</span></li>
                        <li>White background with a red stripe along the top → <span class="quiz">Ohio</span></li>
                        <li>White background with a pale blue top band → <span class="quiz">Minnesota / Montana / Iowa</span></li>
                        <li>White background with blue at the top and yellow at the bottom → <span class="quiz">Pennsylvania</span></li>
                        <li>White background with reddish-brown at the top and blue at the bottom → <span class="quiz">Idaho</span></li>
                        <li>White background with a faint red highlight near the top and a navy serial area → <span class="quiz">California</span></li>
                        <li>Bright blue upper half and white lower half → <span class="quiz">Connecticut</span>; <span class="quiz">Illinois</span> can look similar.</li>
                        <li>White top half with a light blue lower half → <span class="quiz">Kentucky</span></li>
                        <li class="no-evidence">Faint yellow on top and pale blue on the bottom → <span class="quiz">Washington?</span></li>
                        <li>Front plate with a rainbow → <span class="quiz">Hawaii</span></li>
                    </ul>
                </li>
            </ul>
        </li>
        <li>② Once you spot interstate or state-highway shields you can pinpoint your location.
            <ul>
                <li>State-highway shields often give away the state. In some places the shield outline is the shape of the state itself.</li>
                <li>Interstate shields (blue signs with a red crown) use even numbers for east–west routes and odd numbers for north–south routes, with larger numbers in the northeast.</li>
                <li>Exits are numbered (“Exit 1”, etc.) and become visible when you zoom in.</li>
            </ul>
        </li>
        <li>③ The Hawaiian Islands also have Street View, and the soil stands out as dark or reddish{{% ex "https://goo.gl/maps/jtYyZC2QNiC829Hn6" "https://goo.gl/maps/P7nuAhYyR9LTpAxh9" %}}</li>
    </ul>
</div>

    <h4 class="section-title">Other tips</h4>
    <ul class="rule-list">
        <li>When you spawn inside a maze-like subdivision, avoid streets where you can see the back of a STOP sign—the larger road is usually the direction where the front of the sign faces{{% ref "https://www.twitch.tv/saclearchan" "First GeoGuessr Pinpoint Symposium – Dash techniques, memorising US state shields, CoopAOW 25k speedrun" %}}
            <ul>
                <li>{{% goto "https://maps.app.goo.gl/MwTvTegi9C3M5nr38" "Example" %}} You can see the backside of STOP or “NO OUTLET” signs in all directions except southwest. Head southwest and you will soon see the front of a STOP sign to the east, guiding you towards a larger road.</li>
            </ul>
        </li>
        <li class="no-evidence">Petrol stations have different regional footprints{{% ref "https://www.scrapehero.com/location-reports/10-largest-gas-stations-in-the-usa/#3143" "10 Largest Gas Stations in the United States in 2023" %}}
            <ul>
                <li>Chevron → Gulf Coast or West Coast states</li>
                <li>Citgo → Eastern states</li>
                <li>Phillips 66 → Inland states</li>
                <li>Murphy USA → Eastern states</li>
                <li>Texaco → Gulf Coast or West Coast states</li>
            </ul>
        </li>
        <li class="no-evidence">Some states can be identified from vehicle inspection stickers.</li>
    </ul>
