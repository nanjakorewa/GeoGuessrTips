    <h2 class="section-title">{{% i18n "how-to-distinguish" %}}</h2>
    <ul class="rule-list">
        <li>Cars drive on the <span class="quiz">left side</span> of the road.</li>
        <li>Bengali script (বাংলা লিপি) is visible, characterized by the ◁ shape.</li>
        <li>Commercial vehicles have <span class="quiz">green</span> license plates.</li>
        <li>There are signs with <span class="quiz">black and white stripes</span>, similar to those in Bhutan nearby.</li>
        <li>There are many bollards with <span class="quiz">red and white stripes</span>, often found near bridges.</li>
        <li>Baby taxis and CNG rickshaws are running{{% ref "https://www.jftc.jp/monthly/overseas/entry-1748.html" "Japan Foreign Trade Council Monthly Online - Changing Traffic Conditions in Bangladesh" %}}.</li>
        <li>Google Car is distinctive.</li>
    </ul>

    <ul class="rule-list">
        <li>The name of the town is often written on the corner of the Dhaka Bank or Dutch-Bangla Bank sign. If you know the approximate area, this will give you a clue.</li>

{{% tab "Dhaka Bank" %}}

{{% notice tip %}}
When you see signs for Dhaka Bank or Dutch-Bangla Bank, one side should have the town name written in <b>English</b>, so you should be able to read it. The other side is in Bengali, which can be difficult to read.
