<div class="main-desciption area-description">
    <h2 class="section-title">{{% i18n "narrow-down-the-area" %}}</h2>
    <ul class="rule-list">
        <li>You can roughly determine the region from the telephone area code</li>
        <li>Road markings can sometimes indicate the prefecture</li>
        <li><a href="./tohoku/hokkaido/" class="area-link">Hokkaido</a>
            <ul>
                <li>There is a <span class="quiz">Seicomart</span> convenience store</li>
                <li>There are <span class="quiz">snow poles / delineators</span> on the road</li>
                <li>Houses characteristic of cold regions are common
                    <ul>
                        <li>Roofs are <span class="quiz">flat</span></li>
                        <li>There are cascade-style garages</li>
                        <li>Houses with <span class="quiz">kerosene tanks</span> called home tanks are prominent{{% ref "http://www.sundia.co.jp/product/home_equipment/home-tank-main/" "Sundaiya Oil Tank" %}}</li>
                    </ul>
                </li>
            </ul>
        </li>
        <li><a href="./chugoku/" class="area-link">Chugoku region</a>
            <ul>
                <li>Houses using Sekishu roof tiles have <span class="quiz">reddish</span> roofs, found mainly in the San'in region centered around Higashi-Hiroshima</li>
                <li>If the guardrails are the color of <span class="quiz">summer oranges</span>, you are in Yamaguchi Prefecture</li>
            </ul>
        </li>
        <li><a href="./kyusyu/" class="area-link">Kyushu region</a>
            <ul>
                <li>There is a lot of <span class="quiz">pampas grass (susuki)</span>{{% ref "https://www.city.fukuoka.lg.jp/data/open/cnt/3/65125/1/H30sizenmatome.pdf?20210628182402" "Fukuoka City Nature - Mountain Edition" %}}</li>
                <li>Signs in Kumamoto have red tape wrapped around them</li>
                <li>Signs in Miyazaki have yellow tape wrapped around them</li>
                <li class="no-evidence" style="opacity:0.4">The vertical stroke of the character 're' in the road marking 'Tomare' (Stop) may differ in length from other regions?</li>
            </ul>
        </li>
        <li><a href="./kyusyu/okinawa/" class="area-link">Okinawa</a>
            <ul>
                <li>Many one-story buildings</li>
                <li>Many <span class="quiz">white, flat buildings</span></li>
                <li>There are <span class="quiz">water tanks</span> on rooftops</li>
                <li><span class="quiz">"Ishiganto"</span> is written on walls and other surfaces</li>
            </ul>
        </li>
    </ul>
</div>

<div class="main-desciption area-description">
    <h4>Utility Poles and Signs</h4>
    <ul class="rule-list">
        <li>Because power companies and distribution operators differ by region, utility pole logos, plates, and guy-wires have regional characteristics ({{% goto "https://www.plonkit.net/japan" "plonk it Japan page" %}})</li>
        <li>Cold regions like Hokkaido and Tohoku have distinctive features
            <ul>
                <li>Traffic lights are sometimes oriented <span class="quiz">vertically</span> as a snow countermeasure</li>
                <li>Phone booth roofs are sometimes non-flat as a snow countermeasure</li>
                <li class="no-evidence">There are "Stop Line" signs</li>
                <li class="no-evidence">Power lines have anti-galloping dampers{{% ref "https://www.hepco.co.jp/network/stable_supply/efforts/snow_prevention/index.html" "Equipment to Prevent Snow-Related Outages" %}}</li>
            </ul>
        </li>
        <li>Utility pole plates and pole-top shapes differ by region{{% ref "https://docs.google.com/document/d/16lVvh3sLCnHLh_itpEsvXrHHCR_EPzArBmb4bg6TUTU/edit#" "Guide to Regional Plates on Japanese Utility Pole" %}}{{% ref "https://docs.google.com/document/d/1Pm4qV16U5Yp4xMP4PnR_AUY4Td1aB0kPoCEzNRN2ESU/edit" "A Japan guide by Fanty" %}}
            <ul>
                <li class="no-evidence">Horizontal plates are possible in cold regions like Hokuriku and Tohoku</li>
            </ul>
        </li>
        <li>Utility pole guy-wires differ by region{{% ref "https://docs.google.com/document/d/18s_xdyXfVpBNHzwn8PtC9otpIwAJNWXjeeWg2egVd4U/edit" "Regional Differences in Guy-Wires for utility poles in Japan" %}}
        </li>
    </ul>
</div>
