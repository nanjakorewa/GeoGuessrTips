    <h2 class="section-title">{{% i18n "how-to-distinguish" %}}</h2>
    <ul class="rule-list">
        <li>The country-code domain is <span class="quiz">.fr</span></li>
        <li>Phone numbers appear as <span class="quiz">five pairs of digits</span></li>

        <li>You frequently see French characters like <span class="quiz">"â, î, û, ê, ô"</span> and "é"</li>
        <li>Utility poles sometimes carry a <span class="quiz">blue plate</span>{{% by "https://youtu.be/LxWFy5o3dDg?t=138" "youtube" "Geoguessr Tips: Guide to European Utility Poles" %}}</li>

{{% notice tip %}}
Phone numbers consist of five pairs of two-digit numbers. The first pair (essentially the area code) identifies the region. Paris is `01`. From `02` onward they go clockwise, making them easier to remember compared to other countries.
{{% /notice %}}

    <h4 class="section-title">Architecture</h4>
    <ul class="rule-list">

                <li>Major slate-producing areas are the Brittany peninsula in the northwest{{% ex "https://goo.gl/maps/6rgUxzwvaQePYTXW9" "https://goo.gl/maps/y74Bw4UCHG3RNAFZ6" %}}, the Corrèze area in central France{{% ex "https://goo.gl/maps/bRyiRAK3AEGJDDbU9" "https://goo.gl/maps/gez9ghkt7HKxdD4f8" %}}, and near the Pyrenees{{% ex "https://goo.gl/maps/Q1QARGvX2Dp7cA75A" "https://goo.gl/maps/UNnrg6nQkvtZBtkV9" %}} — generally more common in the northern half centered around the peninsula</li>
                <li>{{% ref "https://drive.google.com/file/d/1xgyIFSG1jM2V92cXNUs1K5ZkIAFEKIqt/view" "France Tips for Geoguessr Guillaume Borde (Guigz#3433)" %}}</li>

            </ul>
        </li>
        <li>If you see a lot of brick, consider the area near {{% goto "../belgium/" "Belgium" %}}{{% by "now_and_jenn" "twitch" %}}{{% ref "https://drive.google.com/file/d/1xgyIFSG1jM2V92cXNUs1K5ZkIAFEKIqt/view" "France Tips for Geoguessr Guillaume Borde (Guigz#3433)" %}}</li>
