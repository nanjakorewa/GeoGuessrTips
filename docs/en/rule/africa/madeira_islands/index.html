Madeira Isrands

How to Distinguish

  • A remote island in Portuguese territory, with license plates, signs, and utility poles similar to those found on the Portuguese mainland
  • The island's coastline is dominated by rocky sea cliffs.
  • There are distinctive-looking parapets and bollards made of concrete.
  • The area code is 291
  • Fog tends to form when moist air from the sea meets the trade winds
  • More rugged than the Azores.

    License plates, signs, and utility poles similar to those found on the Portugal mainland .

    Bridge parapets and road fences are made of concrete, sometimes with white blocks on top of them .

    There is a distinctive-looking bollard that appears to be made of concrete . Also, fog tends to occur, and sometimes it is difficult to see in the distance .

    You can find purple and white flowers like this .

    As can be seen from other photos, the island is not flat in topography with hills and valleys formed by weathering and erosion ( Madeira Islands).
